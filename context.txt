 📌 The Problem
India loses an estimated ₹2.17 trillion annually to corruption in public infrastructure projects. The core issues include rampant cost overruns with no accountability, contractors facing zero real consequences for shoddy work (simply rebranding and winning new tenders), disappearing investigation reports, altered approval records, and silenced whistleblowers — all enabled by single-point approval systems with zero transparency.

💡 The Solution — Project Satya
Project Satya is a blockchain-powered accountability platform designed to bring radical transparency and cryptographic trust to India's infrastructure procurement and delivery ecosystem. It leverages decentralized trust networks to make corruption visible, traceable, and financially painful.
Key Features:

Blockchain-Anchored Audit Trail — Every tender decision, approval signature, inspection report, and payment authorization is recorded with cryptographic timestamps on a public ledger, making evidence destruction mathematically impossible.
Smart Contract Performance Bonds — 20% of every project's value is automatically locked in escrow for 5 years, with programmatic slashing triggered by quality failures — removing the need for slow, corruptible bank guarantees.
Multi-Party Cryptographic Approvals — Payment releases require a 4-of-6 digital signature from independent stakeholders (government engineer, insurance company, third-party auditor, community representative, contractor QA, and environmental auditor), eliminating single-point corruption.
Permanent Reputation Ledger — Immutable, career-long performance scores for every contractor and inspector, making shell-company rebranding ineffective.
AI-Powered Risk Scoring & Public Dashboard — A citizen-facing transparency portal with real-time risk detection, empowering 1.4 billion taxpayers to flag corruption before bridges collapse.
